WHAT IS MODERN ART?

Modern Art is the art period roughly between the 1860s and the 1970s. It represented a big change from its traditional predecessors in the styles and techniques used to create art. Modern art is characterized by the rejection of tradition, new materials and techniques, the focus on individual expression and the use of abstraction and symbolism.

THE MOMA

NEW YORK, USA

THE MUSEUM OF MODERN ART

The Museum of Modern Art, commonly known as the MoMA, was founded in 1929 and is located in New York City. Its collection of over 200 000 works comprises paintings, sculptures, photography, design and more, all from the modern and contemporary art periods.

Personally, it is my favourite art museum I have had the opportunity to visit. It took me around 2h to visit the whole museum and it was a great experience of seeing walking around the museum since it is set up as a timeline, so you get to experience the different periods of modern art. I would totally recommend visiting the MoMA to anyone who is a fan of modern art.

THE METROPOLITAN MUSEUM OF ART

The Metropolitan Museum of Art, commonly known as the Met is one of the most famous art museums in the world. Also located in New York City, it was founded in 1870 and has a collection of over 2 millions works from all around the world, including ancient artifacts, sculptures, european paintings, modern works and so much more.

The Met truly is a museum I think any art lover should visit at least once in their life. It is a huge museum that has something for anyone to enjoy, including a whole section dedicated to modern art. Personally, I have been there twice and spent around 6h walking around and I know I could go back and see and discover new things, which is why I love this museum so much.

LONDON, UK

THE COURTAULD GALLERY

Located in London and part of the Somerset House, the Courtauld Gallery is an art museum founded in 1932, containing around 30 000 works known for its collection of European art, specifically Impressionist and Post-Impressionist works.

While in London, we randomly stumbled upon this gallery and it was a great find. The whole museum took around an hour and a half to visit and it was a great way of seeing different modern art works in a more intimate setting, since the museum is much smaller than others but I would still recommend it all the same to anyone who finds a big museum a bit overwhelming.
